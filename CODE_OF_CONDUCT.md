# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in the Estatio community a welcoming,
safe, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, inclusive,
and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and welcoming.
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback.
- Assuming good intentions.
- Focusing on what is best for the community.
- Helping newcomers learn and grow.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.
- Respecting different viewpoints and experiences.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech.
- Personal attacks or insulting language.
- Trolling or deliberately disruptive behavior.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
professional environment.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards
of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or
harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, issues, discussions, and pull requests that are not
aligned with this Code of Conduct.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- GitHub Issues
- GitHub Discussions
- Pull Requests
- Project documentation
- Community chat platforms
- Any public representation of the project

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it privately
to the project maintainers.

You can create a confidential GitHub security report or contact the maintainer
through GitHub.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and fairly.
